It's been a little while, and a few things have happened. I'm now a scientist in the [Wayment-Steele lab](https://waymentsteelelab.org/) at UW-Madison! I'm having a lot of fun setting up some exciting stuff and look forward to showing some of it before long.

There have been a few publications. Matt's work on proton-sensing GPCRs is out in [Cell](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2024.11.036), and both of Gabby's MET kinase studies landed in eLife — the [conserved regulatory motifs](https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.91619) paper and its [inhibitor-resistance mapping](https://doi.org/10.7554/eLife.101882.3) followup. A couple DMS tools have been published, too: Jingyou's [Rosace-AA](https://doi.org/10.1093/bioadv/vbaf218) in Bioinformatics Advances, Jerome's [Lilace](https://doi.org/10.1186/s13059-026-03934-1) in Genome Biology. There are also Pooja's [fragment screening against VatD](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.str.2025.08.017) in Structure, and a [review of high-throughput mutational screens for membrane transporters](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.chembiol.2025.03.003) with Silas and the Raman lab in Cell Chemical Biology.

A preprint about a [suite of codon foundation models](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2024.10.10.617568v1) using our DMS data in collaboration with the Goodarzi lab is up, as well as Donovan's [deep mutational scan of EccD3](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2024.08.23.609456v1) in the mycobacterial ESX secretion system.
