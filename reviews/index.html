reviews

public peer reviews.

Peer review is a good idea. Gatekeeping is not. As a reviewer, I see my job as sharpening the work by defining the boundaries of what can be supported, not dictating what I want the work to be. I aim to be attentive, courteous, and constructive. As a mentor, I try to involve trainees in the review process as well.

See the peer review section of the Fraser lab compact for a lot more on this topic that I agree with, along with the Peer Review in the Life Sciences course we co-taught in 2023. Also see the polemically-titled “Is Peer Review a Good Idea?” by Remco Heesen and Liam Kofi Bright for an interesting argument.
